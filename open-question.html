The open question

Research note · the testable core, in half a page · Jul 2026

The claim

T≡M v2.1 replaces an unobservable universal residual with a differential drift ε_R between clock types. Two arms make the universal part unobservable: an identical shift across all clocks cancels in frequency ratios, and a constant per-species offset is absorbed into calibration. What could remain observable is a drift that differs between clock types, bounded today by metrology at |Δε_R| ≲ 10⁻⁸.

Coherent Data (§19) proposes the link ε_R ≈ λ·χ_R: the residual drift of a clock would be proportional to its coherence susceptibility, ordered by the compactness of its structural closure — nuclear transitions (most compact) least susceptible, extended electronic transitions most.

The clash of orderings

Standard constant-variation physics orders clock transitions by their sensitivity coefficients to α, μ, … — and for the ²²⁹Th nuclear transition it predicts an enhanced sensitivity, with an amplification factor of order 10⁴ and negative sign (Flambaum 2006; Fadeev, Berengut & Flambaum 2020; Beeks et al. 2025). This framework orders the same transitions by closure compactness — nearly the opposite ordering. That near-opposition is exactly what makes a signature distinguishable:

If a differential drift is ever measured and follows the standard α-sensitivity ordering (nuclear large, sign inverted; electronic small), the χ_R hypothesis fails. If it follows closure compactness instead, this framework carries a signature of its own.

The decisive pair is ²²⁹Th against an electronic optical clock — and it is no longer hypothetical: the first direct frequency comparison of the ²²⁹Th nuclear transition against a ⁸⁷Sr clock exists (Zhang et al. 2024), solid-state reproducibility has been characterized (Ooi et al. 2026), and a closed-loop thorium nuclear clock has been demonstrated (TU Wien / PTB, 2026).

The question

Do existing multi-species clock-comparison datasets already bound a residual differential drift ordered by closure compactness rather than by α-sensitivity coefficients — and if so, at what level?

What is missing

A formal, calculable definition of χ_R (today it is a structural notion: compactness of the closed dynamics sustaining a transition). The framework states its falsifier openly and archives every version, including corrections, with a DOI — see the revision history.
